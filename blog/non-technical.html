Confessions of a JavaScript Newbie: A Journey into the Abyss

11 August 2024

Alright, here we go. I’ve been staring at this blank screen, trying to explain JavaScript without sounding completely clueless. Unfortunately, when it comes to this I’m clueless, but I like learning and I like writing, so I’m giving it a shot anyway. But trust me to leave it till the very last moment of the very last day that we have to do it, to do it!! This might be the hardest thing I’ve done, maybe on the hardness scale of "iron"? But not quite "Diamond". Anyway, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS...

  • HTML is like the skeleton of your website—it’s the bones that hold everything together.
  • CSS is the outfit you throw on that skeleton to make it look decent.
  • JavaScript? It’s the brain, the nervous system, the personality.
It makes your skeleton move, dance, and all that funky stuff. Without JavaScript, your webpage is just a mannequin—nice to look at, but not much fun at a party.

Control Flow and Loops:

Like Trying to Remember Your Morning Routine

Control flow is like your morning routine, assuming you can remember it before coffee. Let’s say you’re making breakfast:

  • Check if you have bread. (If yes, continue; if no, curse and go buy some.)
  • Toast the bread. (Stare at the toaster until it’s done.)
  • Butter the toast. (Try not to make a mess.)
  • Eat the toast. (Finally, Break-The-Fast!!!.)
That’s control flow—a sequence of steps. Loops come into play when you’re making breakfast for a group. Instead of buttering each slice manually, you create a loop to do it automatically. Less thinking, more toast.

The JavaScript

The DOM. Arrays. Functions.

The DOM: Your Website’s IKEA Furniture

The DOM, or Document Object Model, is like the IKEA instruction manual for your website. JavaScript is the little Allen wrench you use to put everything together. Want to add a potted plant (a new element) to your bookshelf (the webpage)? JavaScript lets you pick the spot (element) in the DOM and place the plant there. Just hope it doesn’t tip over and spill dirt everywhere.

Arrays vs. Objects: Choosing Your Storage Container

Arrays are like bento boxes—everything’s neat and in order. If you need something, you say, “Give me the second compartment,” and there it is. Objects, though, are more like your junk drawer. The cool thing is, you can label everything. So when you’re hunting for that one random screw, you can just say, “Give me the thing labeled ‘screw,’” and there it is.

Functions: Your Handy-Dandy Multi-Tool

Functions are like the Swiss Army knives of coding. They’re blocks of code you can reuse whenever you need them, saving you from writing the same thing over and over. Imagine you’re making a PB&J sandwich. Instead of manually spreading peanut butter every time, you create a function called spreadPeanutButter(), and let it do the work for you. It’s a small thing, but it adds up.

And there you have it — my attempt at making sense of JavaScript, while also trying not to pull my hair out. Writing this was hard, but I like writing, so it was the good kind of hard. We’re all just trying to make sense of coding, one confusing concept at a time. And even though it took my this whole week to learn all of this, Slow Learning is better than No Learning.
