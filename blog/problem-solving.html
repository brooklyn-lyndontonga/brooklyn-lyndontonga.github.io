Problem Solving:

Problem solving is a crucial skill for any developer. It involves breaking down complex problems into smaller, manageable parts, and then finding a solution to each part. This skill is essential for any developer, as it allows them to tackle complex problems and create efficient solutions.

When Solving a Simple Problem:

There were times where I over complicated things throughout this sprint and would get caught up on the return/results and lose track of the simple things that were pulling me up.

When these issues came up I found the Rubber Ducky method to be quite beneficial, or some variation of the method. I would talk to myself internally instead of talking to inanimate objects - as to not look like I've lost my marbles.

Throughout the process I had to remind myself that I don't know a lot of what there is to know and to give myself grace and that it is okay to ask for help or take a break.

There was more personal development and learning done this week than technical learning but also in that, I think my efforts deserve some credit.

Elegant Problem Solving:

Like I’ve said before I tend to overcomplicate things and this week to solve that particular issue I was able to apply the problem solving technique “Pseudocoding”.

By sectioning off each expectation or goal into its own space, I was able to break each problem into its own little category with cascading actions below to solve the problem.

Also considering that I am somewhat of a dummy and find it hard to retain new knowledge, I found this method extremely beneficial in the sense that within each list of actions I was able to include the necessary research to learn what and how each action can solve the problem.

From this experience and with this new tool I am able to structure my workload better and as a result, solve problems faster.

Reflections: Problem Solving:

After a good amount of research on the suggested problem solving methods I am confident in my ability to practice the methods adequately. I have become quite fond of the Pseudocode method and pairing that with Google to access good sources of information I was able to accomplish the exercises in a good time, while strengthening the neuroplasticity towards web development.
